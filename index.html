Zero News
A tool for distributing news over zero-rated channels

Donovan Hide
April 2013
http://github.com/donovanhide/zeronews

Elevator Pitch

A bold, simple and disruptive tool to disseminate news across zero-rated mobile channels through which news organisations will be able to reach new audiences.


Real world challenge

Currently most news received via mobile costs money in data charges. This is a disincentive to consume news, especially among price-conscious phone users. To reach this demographic, thereby increasing democratic participation, these costs needs to be removed. Smartphones are a luxury item. Featurephones need to be the focus of development.

How is your project different from what others are already doing?

Mxit charges for news using micro-payments and do not have a data deal with networks. Facebook and Google have deals which Zero News intends to piggyback on to disseminate news. A very similar model has been adopted by Wikipedia who have managed to get their URL's zero-rated in numerous countries.


How and why will your solution work?

African news organisations will agree to provide a minimum of 5 news articles a day with a reasonable word count. These articles will be instantaneously broadcast to the Zero Facebook and Google+ streams of subscribers to Zero News at no cost to the user. News organisations can view the analytics of each article and see what news is being discussed and shared and get a real insight into a previously unreachable demographic.

Target audience

Nokia Series 40 and Symbian phones running Opera Mini are the most popular (see next slides) African phone/browser combination. Owners of this level and below are the audience.


Marketing

Involved news organisations would advertise the service through their printed papers, radio stations and web sites. "Zero News: 5 articles a day for free".

Connections

Expressions of interest have been received from The Star Kenya, Mail and Guardian, AllAfrica.com and Media Monitoring Africa. Crucial to success is the buy-in from as many news organisations as possible. An agreement with a pan-African news agency, such as the BBC, could kickstart the interest of others.

Ecosystem

The African Media Initiative itself and the upcoming African Media Leader's Forum are perfect vehicles for persuading news organisations of the logic behind reaching and understanding a wider audience. AllAfrica has established links with a large number of outlets. Facebook and Google have experience of negotiating deals with mobile networks. The non-technical task is to bring all these entities together for a common purpose. A well-connected, Africa-based ambassador/champion is vital for this part of the proposal to succeed.

Measurement of success

At least one news organisation in every African nation with a zero-rated channel providing zero cost news which is widely read and actively discussed and shared.

Implementation (Milestone 1)

  • Fully explore the Facebook Stream and Google Moment/Activity API's to work out the best way to ingest the content into users' streams/walls.
  • Build an Appengine application in Go to allow control of user subscriptions and bundle and distribute news items by country.
  • Recruit testers in countries with zero-rated channel availability to use the beta app and provide feedback.
  • Liase with news organisations to find best ways of providing correctly formatted news content, through one of RSS, Open Graph markup or schema.org markup.

Implementation (Milestone 2)

  • Build a reporting interface so news providers can get feedback on which articles are being read or discussed and integrate Google+ and Facebook Insights analytics data.
  • Translate user interface into other languages (initially French).
  • Sign up news providers in selected countries, perhaps at AMLF 2013.
  • Investigate SMS as a means of news delivery. Unsure if this is required, need more data on phone models in use in covered countries.

Implentation (Post launch)

  • Discuss with Wikipedia partners (Praekalt foundation) how they arranged for URL's to be whitelisted with various mobile operators. See if there is a possibility of getting a URL zero-rated purely for news dissemmination, thereby removing the dependency on Google and Facebook's terms of service and future subsidiy or deal alterations.
  • Investigate possibility of providing channels specifically for citizen reporting.

Oversight and Governance

Donovan Hide will lead the project for the Media Standards Trust (MST). Donovan Hide developed churnalism.com for the MST, and has since worked with the MST and Sunlight Foundation to develop and launch the US version of the service.

Donovan will report to Martin Moore, the director of the Media Standards Trust. Martin Moore has previously led the MST transparency initiative (a Knight News Challenge Award winner), and has managed the development of churnalism.com, journalisted.com, and unsourced.org.

The Media Standards Trust is a company limited by guarantee (Company Number 05514310) and a registered charity (Charity Number 1113680).
